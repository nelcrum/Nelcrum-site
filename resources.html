Resources for nonprofits

The policies every nonprofit should have on the shelf.

Get the full templates →

Funders, auditors, and the IRS expect a baseline set of governance and financial policies, no matter your size or focus area. Use these as a checklist of best practices. Expand each to see what a strong version should cover.

01 Conflict of Interest PolicyKeeps decisions in the organization's interest, not personal gain. +
Should cover
  • Annual written disclosures from board and key staff
  • Clear definition of an "interested person" and "financial interest"
  • Recusal and documentation procedure for conflicts
  • Review process for related-party transactions
02 Spending & Financial Controls PolicyDefines who can authorize, approve, and handle money. +
Should cover
  • Approval thresholds and authorization levels by role
  • Segregation of duties between approving, paying, and recording
  • Credit-card, reimbursement, and check-signing limits
  • Monthly reconciliation and board financial review
03 Procurement & Competitive Bidding PolicyEnsures fair, documented purchasing, and protects grant funds. +
Should cover
  • Dollar thresholds that trigger multiple quotes or formal bids
  • Sole-source justification and approval rules
  • Vendor conflict-of-interest checks and documentation
  • Alignment with federal grant rules (2 CFR 200) where applicable
04 Whistleblower PolicyLets staff report concerns without fear of retaliation. +
Should cover
  • A confidential channel for reporting misconduct
  • Explicit non-retaliation protection
  • Investigation procedure and designated contact
  • Recordkeeping of reports and resolutions
05 Document Retention & Destruction PolicyDefines how long records are kept, and when they're destroyed. +
Should cover
  • A retention schedule by record type
  • Secure destruction methods
  • Legal-hold suspension during litigation or audit
  • Handling of electronic records and backups
06 Gift Acceptance PolicyClarifies which gifts you'll accept, and how you'll handle them. +
Should cover
  • Acceptable gift types (cash, in-kind, stock, planned)
  • Review process for restricted or complex gifts
  • Donor acknowledgment and IRS substantiation
  • Gift agreements for major or restricted gifts
07 Board Governance & BylawsSets the rules for how the board operates and decides. +
Should cover
  • Board roles, terms, and term limits
  • Quorum, voting, and committee structure
  • Officer duties and succession
  • Annual review and documented meeting minutes
08 Investment & Operating Reserve PolicyGuides stewardship of cash and reserves over time. +
Should cover
  • A target operating reserve (often 3 to 6 months)
  • Allowed investment types and risk tolerance
  • Who is responsible for oversight
  • A schedule for periodic review
